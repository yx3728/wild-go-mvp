# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Urban explorers, families, students, and casual nature observers using a phone outdoors. They want a lightweight reason to notice real organisms around them without needing to become expert naturalists first.

## Product Purpose

Wild Go turns a real-world plant or animal photo into an original collectible card. The MVP should make the post-capture moment feel rewarding, make rarity and collection progress legible, and keep location privacy and wildlife safety visible by default.

## Brand Personality

Curious, tactile, and field-ready. The interface should feel like a city nature card binder brought to life, not a generic species identification utility.

## Anti-references

Do not become another plain organism ID app, generic social feed, flat observation list, or franchise card clone. Avoid placeholder art, CSS-only fake assets, decorative motion without state meaning, exact sensitive locations, and card surfaces that read as ordinary panels instead of collectible objects.

## Design Principles

- Treat every observation as a collectible artifact, with rarity visible from the card face.
- Make material and motion do product work: tilt reveals foil, press communicates depth, flip reveals details, and stacks imply sharing.
- Keep wildlife trust explicit through softened locations, confidence copy, and rarity-as-discovery-difficulty language.
- Preserve a dense mobile product surface that can be used while walking, scanning, and comparing cards.
- Use the handoff visual references as the canonical card-system direction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, and card state must remain understandable without shimmer, color alone, or device sensors.

# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Wild Go is for ordinary city residents, students, families, walkers, photographers, and nature-curious friends. They use it during commutes, park walks, neighborhood errands, and weekend outings to turn a nearby plant, animal, insect, or fungus into a personal discovery they can keep and share.

## Product Purpose

Wild Go turns real-world urban nature observation into a collectible game. A user photographs an organism, receives an uncertainty-aware likely match, unlocks a rarity card made from their own photo, adds it to a city binder, and shares or showcases the find. Success means the next walk feels worth exploring because the user's collection, missions, and friendships give everyday nature lasting personal value.

## Brand Personality

Curious, tactile, celebratory. Wild Go should make a real discovery feel special without becoming childish, scientifically overconfident, or detached from responsible wildlife behavior.

## Anti-references

- Another utilitarian species-identification app whose main outcome is a taxonomy result.
- A generic social photo feed where organisms become ordinary posts.
- A direct copy of Pokemon card layouts, marks, type symbols, terminology, or franchise-specific visual language.
- A casino-like rarity system that confuses app discovery difficulty with conservation status.
- Interfaces that expose exact sensitive-species locations or encourage touching, feeding, picking, collecting, or disturbing wildlife.

## Design Principles

- Make the user's own discovery the hero: the photo and the fact that "I found this" matter more than encyclopedia copy.
- Show rarity before explanation: stars, label, material, and finish should communicate a card's tier directly from its face.
- Earn delight through physicality: tilt, press, flip, and showcase interactions reinforce the feeling of owning a real card.
- Be honest about uncertainty: present likely matches, confidence, alternatives, and useful retake guidance instead of pretending identification is certain.
- Protect nature by default: soften sensitive locations and keep concise, species-appropriate observation guidance close to the card.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for product controls and readable content. Rarity cannot depend on color alone; stars and text labels remain present at every tier. Touch targets should be at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels. Motion and foil effects must have a reduced-motion fallback, and no task may require device movement. Identification uncertainty and wildlife guidance use plain language that works for first-time nature observers.
